There is a tradition in Japan since 1995: Every year, one character will be chosen as kanji of the year at Kiyomizu Temple, which represents the events of the entire year.

So I choose the character of my year 2016 is Shi

It means: to start, beginning.

Begin to refine

What is quality?

I started to change my understanding of the definition of quality, from “The traditional software quality model identifies 6 main quality characteristics: ….”[1] to “Quality is value to some person.”[2]

What is my job?

My job title is QA Engineer, as known as, Quality Assurance Engineer.

How am I able to make quality assured? Not really.

“We don’t own quality. We’re helping the people who are responsible for quality and the things that influence it.”[3]

I see myself as a Quality Assistance[4]

My role: Quality Control -> Quality Assurance -> Quality Assistance
Me: Focus product -> Focus process -> Focus team motivation on testing

— @KevCui November 4, 2016

What is testing?

“Checking Is Confirmation. Testing Is Exploration and Learning.”[5]

I see testing is a way to explore unknown of the product and to provide information about the awareness of risks.

Nice metaphor! I do my best to provide correct relative info to who need know risks. Based on info, not me, they take care of their sleep... https://t.co/Vuofazss3G

— @KevCui November 28, 2016

Begin to be “Crazy”

“Crazy” is being out of the ordinary. It’s a courage to have breakthrough thinking. I knew that some experiments sound “crazy” but I really did them:

Don’t open new bug ticket immediately until must-have

When I have some findings, I’d like to check with other team members first, instead of logging it into bug tracking tool. Why? Because there is no value added to log a bug, fix it does. If its severity is low, regarding effort and outcome, it won’t be fixed; If its severity is high, we should eliminate it immediately.[6]

Another good thing without a bug ticket: I have to talk to developers and show them how the issue looks like. Then we could start to discuss what’s the correct behavior from the programming perspective and from the user perspective. Then we go for a fix and test the fix together. For me, until now it’s the most efficient way to fix bugs.

Remove manual regression tests because I’m shifted-left[7]

No manual regression tests don’t mean not verify bug fix at all. The idea is old “Do testing as early as possible.” Instead of checking fix after developer says “done”, all manual tests should be done earlier before integration. It makes the feedback circle smaller. Let’s say “done” together.

Make team testing great with lots of fun

I say goodbye to the boring team testing. It’s not my style to organize a traditional team testing. I urge to make it funny.

1/2 Testing should be full of fun, like a treasure hunting, an Easter egg search, a Geocache... a game to enjoy to have fun, not to win it

2/2 I'd like to make a Team Testing:
New features over Whole product
User nature act over Planned step
Motivation driven over Result driven

— @KevCui November 4, 2016

Ignore low usage OS version in order to gain precious time

I removed a target OS version in scope in test plan because of its user coverage is really low. It turned to be a really big mistake. 😶

What I learnt:
1. Never ignore an OS if it's still in scope
2. Still perform some tests on low coverage OS to at least check main functions

— @KevCui November 28, 2016

I’ll Fail friendly, Learn fast and Stay strong!
