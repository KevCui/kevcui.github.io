Please Don't Call Me QA


Photo by Gabriel

Before we dive deep into something serious, relax, let’s start with a joke:

How many QA does it take to change a light bulb? None, we just report the darkness.

— RoboCoonie™ | Fluffo™ (@RoboCoonie) December 24, 2008

Funny? True? Now, let me tell you a truth from the darkness: I don’t like people calling me QA, especially those who know me.

I have my strong opinions on it, that’s why I’d like to share my opinions and to express my feeling in this post.

QA is a confusing job title

QA as a profession, its job title is never clear. Open any job search website, you will see “QA” jobs, like:

QA Engineer
QA Tester
QA Test Engineer
QA Automation Engineer
QA Automation Specialist
Manual QA Tester
Manual QA Test Engineer
Automated QA Engineer
Automated QA Test Engineer
...

If you have the time to read all those different job descriptions, the same job title is not necessarily doing a similar job. Moreover, different job titles can mean the same job. For those jobs, there is no need to put “QA” word in the title and they get confusing anyway.

Ok, you may call me QA, what do you expect my job is?

QA is an obsolete practice

Quality assurance per se is a quality management practice brought from the manufacturing industry. Adopting this concept in software development, it starts with QA, then QA team, then Quality Gate… Indeed, this practice can still make sense in some industries which need strict risk management and serious quality management due to the regulations and laws.

However, speaking of myself, I can’t see myself willing to work in those companies and industries in the future. Looking at the big picture from now to the future, more and more IT companies practice towards to customer-focused and fast-paced software development. In those companies, I barely can see where Quality Gates can fit, where the QA team can fit, where a QA can fit. For me, QA is an obsolete practice in software development.

Ok, you may call me QA, what do you expect my day-to-day work looks like?

QA is an unhealthy mindset

In a typical work environment, what developers think of QA?

QA is responsible for quality.
QA is responsible for testing.
QA is nitpicking.
QA is the blocker.
...

What QAs think of developers?

Developer cannot build quality product.
Developer cannot test properly.
Developer is careless.
Developer is the troublemaker.
...

This mindset causes frustrations: Others see QA as the crow, which brings bad news. QAs see themselves as underdogs, which usually loses arguments against developers…

This mindset causes prejudgments: “You are a QA, so why you didn’t catch this bug before it went into production?…”

This mindset causes selfishness: “I don’t test, QA will test.” “My role is to report this issue and it’s not my responsibility to help developers to fix it…”

Ok, you may call me QA, what do you expect my mindset is?

Alright, what should I call you then?

Please simply call me “tester”, because I value:

  • Product quality is build-in, and it cannot be test-out. Therefore, the whole team is responsible for product quality.

  • Testability is much more important than quality management. Therefore, each team member is obliged to think about testing.

  • Testing happens in any time in everywhere of the software development life cycle. Therefore, everyone is accountable for testing.

I do see myself in a position of supporting and coaching team members on software testing. My career goal is to remove the necessity of QA role in the team. Overall, I don’t like QA, it’s not my style.

Ok, after reading this post if you still want to call me QA, please do so.
